LCA 2016 Open Source and Bioinformatics

Call for Papers

The first Open Source and Bioinformatics Miniconf is being held on 1st February 2016 at LCA 2016 in Geelong, Victoria.

This miniconf will make topics in bioinformatics accessible to a general LCA audience. Attendees will learn about the intersection between bioinformatics research and the use and development of open source software.

Members of the bioinformatics community are invited to submit proposals. Please keep in mind that your talk must be targeted at a technical but mostly non-biological audience!

Desired topics include:

  • Bioinformatics tool development

  • IT and systems support for bioinformatics

  • Clinical bioinformatics applications for open source

  • Bioinformatics training for biologists or programmers

  • Pipeline development

  • Working within the bioinformatics ecosystem (e.g. Bioconductor, etc.)

  • Your research project!

How to Submit

Submit your proposal via email to alan.rubin@wehi.edu.au

Please direct questions to the above email address, or ask on Twitter

Tweet to @rubin_af

Your proposal should include:

  • Talk title

  • Abstract (250 words or fewer)

  • Short presenter bio, emphasizing your experiences with open source, IT, or other common LCA topics if applicable

In your proposal, please relate your talk to the broader topics covered by LCA 2016.

The deadline for submissions is Monday, 30 November 2015.
